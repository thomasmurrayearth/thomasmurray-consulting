How I Work

Three things.
Rarely all in one person.

01

Find the problem before solving it

Most difficult work fails not because of bad execution but because the problem itself was never clearly defined. I navigate genuinely ill-defined challenges — the kind where you don't yet know what you're solving — and guide teams through figuring out what they're actually trying to do. The problem itself wasn't yet defined is a description of the starting condition for some of my best work.

02

Choreograph without a title

Delivery in complex, multi-organisation environments rarely follows reporting lines. I drive results by building coalitions and trust — through credibility, listening, and genuine relationships — not by outranking people. This means I can operate effectively in matrix environments, across sector boundaries, and in situations where nobody controls everything.

03

Build what doesn't yet exist

Across every role: I identify something that needs to exist, build a version of it, and show why it matters. The EPMO at Ministry for the Environment. AI-optimised operational processes at the FSA. The Climate Response Accelerator. The pattern is consistent. I don't wait to be asked. I find the gap, build the thing, and demonstrate the value.

"
